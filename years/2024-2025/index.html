Oct 18 2024

Choose Your Own Adventure!

Chris Grossack

When I was a kid I used to love books with branching storylines, where you could flip to different pages in order to read different possible stories. I hope to give you all the same sense of interactivity by presenting a talk that branches! At the start of the seminar, I'll ask for a vote in order to decide between four possible topics: 1. applications of topos theory, 2. an introduction to condensed mathematics, 3. what are infinity categories, and why should you care?, and 4. topological quantum field theories and you. Depending on the topic, there may be other points in the talk where we will have further votes for further branches, for instance in the topos theory talk we could give applications to either algebra, analysis, or logic. I can't wait to see you there!

Oct 11 2024

Mild Solutions of the Navier-Stokes Equations

Satish Chandran

One way to construct solutions to the Navier-Stokes equations is to treat the nonlinearity as a perturbation of the linear part iteratively. Solutions constructed like this are called mild solutions. In this talk, we'll discuss how mild solutions are formally build and some fun facts about them.
